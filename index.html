Victoria's
sports story
is bigger than
you think.

Exploring participation, gender, age and community sport culture across Victoria's six major sports.

01

PARTICIPATION

The sport Victorians think dominates.

AFL may be our cultural icon, but when it comes to getting on the field, more Victorians choose other sports.

🏀 Basketball is Victoria's most-played sport, while football/soccer and cricket are among the few sports still growing.

02

GENDER

Men and women are playing different games.

While Victorians love sport, men and women are often playing different games. Tennis is one of the few sports where participation is relatively balanced.

🎾 Tennis is the most gender-balanced sport in Victoria, while netball and cricket sit at opposite ends of the gender divide.

03

AGE

What happens to sport participation as people age?

Sport participation declines with age, but not all sports follow the same path. While basketball and soccer lose players rapidly, tennis remains popular across every stage of life.

🌟 Tennis is the only major sport that maintains strong participation across every age group, including adults aged 65 and over.

04

COMMUNITY

AFL wins off the field.

Participation tells only part of the story. AFL may not have the most players, but it has the strongest volunteer network in Victoria.

🏆 AFL attracts more volunteers than any other major sport, highlighting its unique role in Victoria's community sporting culture.

05

ACROSS VICTORIA

Sport reaches every corner of Victoria.

Sport reaches every corner of Victoria, but some sports leave a larger footprint than others. Cricket facilities are the most widespread across the state, appearing in both metropolitan and regional communities.

06

CONCLUSION

Sport is more than just a game.

Victoria's sporting culture is shaped by participation, gender, age, volunteering and local facilities.

THE BIG PICTURE

Sport is not just played in Victoria — it helps define Victoria.

From basketball's high participation to AFL's volunteer strength, tennis' lifelong appeal and cricket's wide facility footprint, sport connects people across communities in different ways.
